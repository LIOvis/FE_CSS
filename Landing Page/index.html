Invader Zim

Overview

Invader Zim is an animated dark comedy and sci-fi series created by Jhonen Vasquez. Premiering on Nickelodeon in 2001, the show follows the misadventures of Zim, an overzealous but incompetent alien from the Irken Empire. Sent to Earth on a bogus mission (basically to get rid of him), Zim is determined to conquer the planet and prove his worth to the almighty Irken leaders known as the Tallest.

To blend in, Zim disguises himself (poorly) as a human child and enrolls in school, where he clashes constantly with Dib, a paranormal investigator who sees through his disguise and tries to expose him. Zim’s plans for world domination are also repeatedly foiled by his own clueless robot sidekick, GIR, whose chaotic antics often sabotage Zim’s efforts.

The show is known for its twisted humor, grotesque visuals, and satirical take on alien invasion tropes. While it was short-lived, it has gained a massive cult following for its offbeat characters, distinctive art style, and hilarious cynicism.

Additional Information

Creator

Jhonen Vasquez is an American cartoonist, comic book writer, and animator known for his dark, satirical humor and distinctive, highly stylized artwork. Born on September 1, 1974, in San Jose, California, Vasquez developed a fascination with horror and surrealism early on, which would later heavily influence his work.

He first gained attention with the self-published comic Johnny the Homicidal Maniac (1995), a darkly comedic, ultra-violent series about a disturbed serial killer. This was followed by similarly twisted works like Squee!, I Feel Sick, and Fillerbunny, which cemented Vasquez’s reputation for black humor, edgy social commentary, and gothic-inspired visuals.

In 2001, Vasquez brought his singular vision to television with Invader Zim, a children’s animated series that pushed the boundaries of what could air on Nickelodeon. Despite its short run, Invader Zim became a cult classic thanks to Vasquez’s unique sense of humor, offbeat characters, and willingness to embrace the bizarre.

Vasquez has continued to dabble in various projects, from comic book work to graphic design and occasional animation. While not as publicly prolific as some creators, he remains a beloved figure in the alternative comics and animation scene, respected for his irreverent, pitch-black wit and creative world-building.

Comics

The Invader Zim comic series is an officially licensed continuation of the original 2001–2006 animated TV show Invader Zim. Published by Oni Press, the comic launched in July 2015 and ran until 2020, spanning 50 regular issues along with some special editions.

Written and overseen by series creator Jhonen Vasquez, the comics bring back the weird, satirical tone of the show, reuniting fans with Zim, his malfunctioning robot sidekick GIR, and his ever-vigilant human nemesis Dib. The comics maintain the darkly comedic style of the show, mixing outlandish sci-fi plots with Vasquez’s trademark snark and visual flair.

The comic issues often feature standalone misadventures, with storylines like Zim’s attempt to conquer Earth using increasingly absurd methods, GIR’s chaotic schemes, or Dib’s futile efforts to expose Zim’s alien identity. Despite their episodic nature, the comics also explore new alien species and Irken technology, expanding on the TV show’s world in a fun and twisted way.

The Invader Zim comic series was praised for capturing the spirit of the original while giving fans more of the grim, hilarious mayhem they loved. It remains a beloved companion to the cult classic show, especially for fans who never quite got enough of Zim’s doomed invasion efforts.
